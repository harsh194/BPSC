üìú Jahandar Shah's Key Policies ‚ñº

üïå Abolition of Jizya Tax (1713 CE)

Jahandar Shah abolished the Jizya tax in 1713

Jizya Tax: A religious tax imposed on non-Muslims during Mughal rule

Purpose: Aimed at gaining the support of non-Muslims and softening Mughal religious policy

Significance: Marked a departure from the orthodox policies of Aurangzeb

üí∞ Introduction of Ijara System

Jahandar Shah promoted the Ijaredari (Ijara) system in land revenue administration

System: Land revenue was collected through public auction (bidding) and assigned to the highest bidder (contractor)

Impact: Often led to exploitation of peasants, as revenue farmers sought maximum profit

Significance: Showed financial weakness and decline in administrative efficiency
